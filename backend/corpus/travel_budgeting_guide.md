# International Travel Budgeting Guide

## Overview
Budgeting for international travel requires understanding costs across several categories: accommodation, food, transportation, activities, visas, travel insurance, and contingency funds. This guide covers how to estimate and manage travel costs across different budget tiers and destination types.

## Budget Tiers by Destination Region

### Budget Destinations (Southeast Asia, Central America, Eastern Europe, South Asia)
These regions offer excellent value for money. Daily budgets of $25–60 USD are achievable.

**Countries:** Thailand, Vietnam, Cambodia, Indonesia, Guatemala, Mexico (outside resort areas), Romania, Bulgaria, India, Nepal.

- **Accommodation:** $5–20/night (hostels, guesthouses, budget hotels)
- **Food:** $3–10/day (street food, local restaurants, market meals)
- **Transport:** $5–15/day (local buses, ride-hailing apps, ferries)
- **Activities:** $5–20/day (temples, national parks, guided tours)

**Key strategy:** Eat where locals eat. Street food is often fresher, more delicious, and a fraction of tourist restaurant prices.

### Mid-Range Destinations (Southern Europe, South America, Caribbean)
Daily budgets of $80–150 USD cover comfortable travel.

**Countries:** Spain, Portugal, Greece, Italy (outside peak season), Peru, Colombia, Costa Rica, Cuba.

- **Accommodation:** $60–120/night (3-star hotels, boutique guesthouses)
- **Food:** $20–40/day (café breakfasts, restaurant lunches or dinners)
- **Transport:** $15–30/day (trains, buses, occasional taxi/Uber)
- **Activities:** $20–50/day (museum admissions, day tours, cooking classes)

**Key strategy:** Eat lunch at restaurants (usually better value than dinner with set "menú del día" options) and shop at supermarkets for breakfast and snacks.

### Expensive Destinations (Western Europe, North America, Australia, Scandinavia, Japan)
Daily budgets of $150–300+ USD are needed for comfort.

**Countries:** France, Switzerland, Norway, UK, USA, Canada, Australia, Japan, New Zealand.

- **Accommodation:** $120–250/night (mid-range hotels)
- **Food:** $40–80/day (restaurant meals)
- **Transport:** $20–60/day (public transit passes, intercity trains)
- **Activities:** $30–80/day (major museum admissions, day excursions)

**Key strategy:** Book accommodation and intercity transport well in advance. Use city passes (Paris Museum Pass, London Oyster, etc.) if visiting multiple paid attractions. Cook occasionally if accommodation has kitchen access.

## Cost Categories to Budget For

### Flights
International flights are typically the largest single expense. Book 6–12 weeks in advance for best prices (domestic USA: 3–6 weeks). Use Google Flights or Skyscanner to track prices. Consider positioning flights (budget carrier to a hub, then long-haul) for savings. January–February and late October–November are typically cheapest for transatlantic flights.

### Accommodation Types & Costs
- **Hostels (dorm):** $15–40/night — budget travel, social atmosphere, mostly young travelers
- **Hostels (private room):** $30–70/night — hostel facilities with more privacy
- **Guesthouses/B&Bs:** $40–100/night — often family-run, local character
- **3-star hotels:** $80–180/night — reliable comfort, usually breakfast available
- **Boutique hotels:** $150–350/night — design-focused, unique character
- **5-star hotels:** $300–1,000+/night — luxury amenities, premium locations

### Food Budget Strategies
- Markets and supermarkets: 30–50% savings vs. restaurants
- Lunch menus (France, Spain, Italy): Full meal for €10–15 that would cost €30+ at dinner
- Street food and food halls: High quality, low cost in most countries
- Cooking for yourself: Significant savings on longer trips

### Travel Insurance
Non-negotiable for international travel. Costs 4–8% of total trip cost, or roughly $5–15/day. Covers: trip cancellation, medical emergencies (most important — overseas medical bills can be catastrophic), emergency evacuation, lost luggage. Compare policies on InsureMyTrip, Squaremouth, or World Nomads.

### Visas & Entry Fees
- Schengen Area (most of Europe): Free for many nationalities (up to 90 days). ETIAS starting 2025: €7.
- USA ESTA: $21 USD. Valid 2 years.
- India e-Visa: $25–100 USD depending on nationality/duration.
- Kenya e-Visa: $50 USD.
- Australia ETA/eVisitor: $20 AUD or free depending on nationality.

## Currency & Money Tips
- **Notify your bank** before travel to avoid card freezes.
- **Use a no-foreign-transaction-fee credit card** for most purchases (Charles Schwab, Chase Sapphire, Revolut, Wise).
- **ATMs:** Use bank ATMs rather than airport or standalone kiosks (higher fees). Withdraw larger amounts less frequently.
- **Cash:** Carry some local currency for small vendors, rural areas, and tips. Not all destinations are card-friendly.
- **Currency exchange:** Avoid airport exchange desks (poor rates). Use ATMs at destination for best rates. Wise/Revolut apps offer excellent exchange rates.

## Sample Multi-Destination Budget (2 weeks, moderate tier)
- Round-trip flights: $800–1,200
- Accommodation (14 nights × $100): $1,400
- Food (14 days × $40): $560
- Local transport: $200
- Activities & tours: $300
- Travel insurance: $80
- Miscellaneous/contingency (10%): $330
- **Total: approximately $3,670–4,070 USD per person**

Couples typically save 20–30% per person on accommodation costs by sharing rooms.
