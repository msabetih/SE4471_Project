# Greece Travel Guide

## Overview
Greece is a country of ancient history, stunning island landscapes, and Mediterranean warmth. From the Acropolis of Athens and the volcanic caldera of Santorini to the crystal-clear waters of the Ionian Islands and the monasteries of Meteora, Greece offers incredible variety in a relatively compact geography (excluding its 227 inhabited islands).

## Top Destinations

### Athens
The cradle of Western civilization. The Acropolis — crowned by the Parthenon — is one of the world's greatest ancient monuments. Buy a combined ticket (€30) covering the Acropolis, the Ancient Agora, Kerameikos, and several other sites. The Acropolis Museum at the foot of the hill is world-class. The Monastiraki flea market and Plaka neighborhood (traditional tavernas, neoclassical architecture) are excellent for strolling. The Athens food scene has improved dramatically — try the Central Market (Varvakios Agora) for fresh produce and local dishes.

### Santorini
The most iconic Greek island — dramatic caldera cliffs, white-washed buildings with blue domes, and extraordinary sunsets. Oia village is the most photographed spot. Fira is the main town. The black sand beaches of Kamari and Perissa are unique. Santorini is expensive and extremely crowded in July–August; May–June and September–October are better. Ferry from Athens (Piraeus) takes 5–8 hours; flights take 45 minutes.

### Mykonos
Famous for glamorous nightlife, beautiful beaches, and windmills. Little Venice waterfront, Matogiannia (main shopping street), and Paradise Beach are key spots. More affordable than its reputation suggests if you avoid the ultra-luxury end. Connected to Santorini by regular ferries.

### Crete
Greece's largest island is a destination in itself. Heraklion has the Archaeological Museum (Minoan collection) and the Palace of Knossos. The Samaria Gorge (16km hike through Europe's longest gorge) is a classic Crete experience. The Venetian harbor of Chania is beautiful. Vai Beach has Europe's largest natural palm forest. Crete is large enough to require a car to explore properly.

### Meteora
A UNESCO World Heritage site of extraordinary monasteries perched atop towering sandstone pillars in central Greece. Six monasteries remain active. Sunrise and sunset visits are magical. Accessible by train from Athens (4.5 hours to Kalambaka) or as part of a northern Greece itinerary.

### Thessaloniki
Greece's second city is underrated and beloved by Greeks. Excellent food scene (city claims the best food in Greece), Byzantine churches, the White Tower, and the vibrant Ladadika and Valaoritou neighborhoods. A great base for Meteora, Mount Olympus, and the Halkidiki peninsula beaches.

## Transportation
- **Ferries:** The main way to travel between islands. Hellenic Seaways, Blue Star Ferries, and Seajets are major operators. Book via ferryhopper.com or directferries.com.
- **Domestic flights:** Olympic Air and Aegean Airlines connect Athens to major islands quickly. Worth it for distant islands like Rhodes, Corfu, or Lesbos.
- **KTEL buses:** Public buses connect mainland cities. Athens to Thessaloniki (5–6 hours), Athens to Meteora (4.5 hours).
- **Car rental:** Essential for Crete, Rhodes, and mainland exploration. Roads can be narrow and winding.
- **Athens Metro:** 3 lines, clean and efficient. Line 3 connects the airport to the city center in 40 minutes.

## Budget Estimates (per day)
- Budget: €40–60 — hostels and budget rooms, gyros and souvlaki (€2–4), free beach access, combined site tickets
- Moderate: €90–140 — mid-range hotels, taverna meals, ferry tickets between islands
- Luxury: €250+ — boutique caldera hotels in Santorini, sunset dining, private sailing charters

## Best Time to Visit
- **May–June:** Ideal — warm and sunny, lower prices, fewer crowds. Wildflowers in bloom.
- **September–October:** Second best — summer crowds gone, sea still warm for swimming.
- **July–August:** Peak season — very hot (35–40°C on islands), extremely crowded in Santorini and Mykonos, highest prices. Meltemi wind can be strong.
- **November–April:** Many island businesses close. Athens and Thessaloniki remain active year-round. Meteora is beautiful and uncrowded in winter.

## Visa & Entry
Greece is in the Schengen Area. EU/EEA citizens travel freely. Citizens of the USA, Canada, UK, Australia, and most Western countries can visit visa-free for up to 90 days within any 180-day period. ETIAS authorization will be required starting 2025.

## Cultural Tips
- Greeks are warm and hospitable — accepting an offer of coffee or a small gift is polite.
- Dress modestly at churches and monasteries (cover shoulders and knees).
- Siesta (14:00–17:30) is still observed in smaller towns — shops and businesses may close.
- Tipping: 10% in restaurants is appreciated but not obligatory. Round up taxi fares.
- "Opa!" is not just a movie cliché — it genuinely expresses joy and celebration.
- Haggling is not common in shops but asking for a discount ("Ehete ekptosi?") is sometimes effective.
