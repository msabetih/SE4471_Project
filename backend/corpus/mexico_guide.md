# Mexico Travel Guide

## Overview
Mexico is one of the most visited countries in the world, offering ancient Mayan and Aztec ruins, colonial cities, world-class beaches on two coastlines, and some of the planet's most celebrated cuisine. Its incredible diversity — from the Copper Canyon to the Yucatán Peninsula's cenotes — makes it endlessly rewarding.

## Top Destinations

### Mexico City (CDMX)
One of the world's great megacities with a rich cultural life. The Historic Center (Centro Histórico) contains the Zócalo (main plaza), the Metropolitan Cathedral, and the ruins of Templo Mayor (the main Aztec temple). The world-class Museo Nacional de Antropología in Chapultepec Park is a must. The neighborhoods of Roma and Condesa have excellent cafés, restaurants, and street art. Xochimilco's colorful trajinera boats are a uniquely Mexican experience. The floating gardens of Teotihuacán (40km away) with its Pyramid of the Sun are a half-day trip.

### Oaxaca
A colonial city and food lover's paradise. Oaxacan cuisine is one of Mexico's most celebrated regional cuisines: mole negro, tlayudas, tasajo, and mezcal (the state produces most of Mexico's mezcal). The Zapotec ruins of Monte Albán overlook the city. The Saturday market in Tlacolula and the black pottery village of San Bartolo Coyotepec are excellent day trips. Día de los Muertos (Day of the Dead) celebrations in late October/early November are extraordinary here.

### The Yucatán Peninsula

**Chichen Itzá:** One of the New Seven Wonders of the World — the pyramid of El Castillo is iconic. Go early morning before the crowds and heat arrive.

**Tulum:** Dramatic Mayan ruins perched on coastal cliffs above the Caribbean. The town has grown rapidly into a boutique resort destination. Nearby cenotes (natural sinkholes) are extraordinary for swimming and diving.

**Cenotes:** The Yucatán has over 6,000 cenotes. Gran Cenote and Dos Ojos near Tulum, and Ik Kil near Chichén Itzá are popular. Cenote diving is a world-class experience.

**Mérida:** The white city — a well-preserved colonial capital with excellent food and the best base for exploring Yucatán ruins.

### The Riviera Maya & Cancún
Cancún is a resort strip with beautiful Caribbean water — ideal for sun-and-beach travelers. Playa del Carmen is more authentic with 5th Avenue for strolling. Isla Mujeres and Cozumel (excellent diving) are nearby islands. Bacalar, a multi-hued freshwater lagoon, is a quieter alternative.

## Transportation
- **Domestic flights:** Affordable via VivaAerobus, Volaris, and Aeromexico. Essential for covering Mexico's large distances.
- **ADO buses:** First-class coach network, comfortable and reliable for Yucatán, Oaxaca, and southern Mexico.
- **Mexico City Metro:** One of the world's most extensive, very cheap ($0.25 CAD per ride).
- **Colectivos:** Shared minivans in the Yucatán are the cheapest way to travel between towns.
- **Uber:** Available in major cities and often safer/cheaper than street taxis.

## Budget Estimates (per day)
- Budget: $25–45 CAD — guesthouses, tacos and market meals, colectivos, cenotes
- Moderate: $70–120 CAD — mid-range hotels, restaurant meals, guided tours
- Luxury: $200+ CAD — boutique hotels, fine dining, cenote private access, helicopter tours

## Best Time to Visit
- **November–April (dry season):** Best overall. Mild temperatures in highland cities, calm Caribbean seas.
- **May–October (wet season):** Afternoon showers, lush green landscapes. Hurricane risk on Caribbean coast June–October (peak August–October).
- Oaxaca: October/November for Día de los Muertos. December for Christmas posadas.
- Whale shark season near Isla Holbox: June–September.

## Visa & Entry
Citizens of the USA, Canada, UK, EU, and Australia do not need a visa for tourist stays up to 180 days. A Forma Migratoria Múltiple (FMM) tourist card is issued on arrival (sometimes included in airfare, otherwise ~$25 CAD). Keep your FMM — you'll need it when departing.

## Safety & Tips
- Safety varies significantly by region. Popular tourist areas (Yucatán, Oaxaca, CDMX tourist zones, Riviera Maya) are generally safe.
- Use Uber over street taxis in Mexico City; book radio taxis or hotel taxis elsewhere.
- Drink only bottled or filtered water. Montezuma's Revenge (traveler's diarrhea) is common; consider probiotics before traveling.
- Bargaining is appropriate in markets but not in established restaurants or shops.
- Learn basic Spanish — outside major tourist areas, English is limited.
- Tipping: 10–15% in restaurants, 5–10 pesos per bag for porters.
