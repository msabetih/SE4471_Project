# United States Travel Guide

## Overview
The United States is a vast country of extraordinary geographic and cultural diversity — from the skyscrapers of New York City and the national parks of the American West to the beaches of Florida, the jazz clubs of New Orleans, and the tech corridors of California. A single trip can barely scratch the surface; most visitors focus on one or two regions.

## Top Destinations

### New York City
The most visited city in the USA. Manhattan's highlights include Central Park, the Metropolitan Museum of Art (pay-what-you-wish), Times Square, the Brooklyn Bridge (walk across), the High Line, Chelsea Market, and the 9/11 Memorial. The Statue of Liberty requires a ferry (book in advance). Brooklyn's DUMBO neighborhood and Williamsburg are great for food and atmosphere. The subway runs 24/7.

### California
- **San Francisco:** The Golden Gate Bridge (walk or cycle), Alcatraz (book weeks in advance), Fisherman's Wharf, Chinatown, the Castro, and Napa Valley wine country (1.5 hours north) are highlights. A city of steep hills best explored on foot or by cable car.
- **Los Angeles:** Hollywood sign, Griffith Observatory, Getty Center (free), Santa Monica Pier, Venice Beach boardwalk, and the Getty Villa in Malibu. Requires a car — distances are vast.
- **Yosemite National Park:** Stunning granite cliffs, waterfalls (best in spring), and giant sequoias. Book accommodation and permits months in advance for summer.

### National Parks (American West)
The "Big Five" of the Southwest: Grand Canyon (Arizona), Zion (Utah), Bryce Canyon (Utah), Arches (Utah), and Monument Valley (Utah/Arizona). The Utah national parks are ideally combined into a 7–10 day road trip. An "America the Beautiful" annual pass ($80) covers admission to all national parks.

### New Orleans
One of America's most unique cities with French, Spanish, and African influences. The French Quarter's Bourbon Street is lively but Frenchmen Street has better live jazz. The city has exceptional food: beignets at Café Du Monde, gumbo, po'boys, and crawfish étouffée. Mardi Gras (February/March) is the world-famous festival.

### Washington D.C.
The capital's National Mall museums (Smithsonian Institution) are free and world-class: Natural History, American History, Air and Space, and the National Gallery of Art. The Lincoln Memorial, Jefferson Memorial, and Vietnam Veterans Memorial are open 24/7. The Capitol building offers free guided tours (book online).

## Transportation
- **Domestic flights:** The most practical way to cover large distances. Southwest, Delta, United, American, and budget carriers like Spirit/Frontier. Book early for best fares.
- **Amtrak:** Train network is limited compared to Europe but scenic routes exist (California Zephyr, Coast Starlight, Empire Builder). Useful for Northeast Corridor (Boston–NYC–Washington).
- **Car rental:** Essential outside of major cities, especially for national parks, road trips, and the South.
- **Uber/Lyft:** Available in virtually all cities.

## Budget Estimates (per day)
- Budget: $80–120 USD — hostels (limited outside major cities), fast-casual food, free museums
- Moderate: $180–280 USD — mid-range hotels, sit-down restaurants, paid attractions and tours
- Luxury: $400+ USD — boutique hotels, fine dining, private experiences

## Best Time to Visit
- **Northeast (NYC, D.C.):** April–June (spring) and September–October (fall) — mild temperatures, fall foliage.
- **California:** April–October for most areas. Yosemite best May–June (waterfalls). LA is year-round pleasant.
- **Florida:** November–April (avoid summer heat/humidity and hurricane season June–November).
- **Southwest/National Parks:** March–May and September–November. Summer (June–August) is extremely hot (Grand Canyon can reach 40°C+).
- **New Orleans:** October–May. Mardi Gras falls in February or March.

## Visa & Entry
The USA requires visas for many nationalities. Citizens of 42 countries can apply for an ESTA (Electronic System for Travel Authorization) online for visa-free entry for up to 90 days under the Visa Waiver Program (eligible countries include UK, EU, Australia, Japan, South Korea). Others need a B-2 tourist visa from a US embassy. Apply well in advance.

## Cultural Tips
- Tipping is mandatory culture in the USA: 18–20% at restaurants, $1–2 per drink at bars, $1–5 per bag for hotel porters.
- Sales tax is not included in displayed prices — added at checkout (varies by state, typically 5–10%).
- The drinking age is 21 — ID will be checked.
- Many US cities have a car-centric layout — walking between attractions may not always be practical.
- Emergency number is 911 for police, fire, and medical.
