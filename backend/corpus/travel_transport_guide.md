# International Travel Transportation Guide

## Overview
Choosing the right transportation modes can make or break a trip — affecting both cost and experience. This guide covers major transportation options for international travelers across different regions and journey types.

## Air Travel

### Booking Strategy
- **Book 6–12 weeks ahead** for international flights (transatlantic, transpacific). Last-minute international fares are rarely cheap.
- **For domestic/regional flights** within a destination country: 3–6 weeks is often sufficient; budget carriers frequently have sales.
- **Best days to fly:** Tuesday and Wednesday are typically cheapest. Friday and Sunday are most expensive.
- **Cheapest months for transatlantic:** January, February, late October, November (avoiding holidays).
- **Tools:** Google Flights (price tracking), Skyscanner (multi-city searches), Hopper (price prediction).

### Airline Tiers by Region
- **Europe:** Ryanair, easyJet, Wizz Air for budget intra-Europe flights ($20–80 one-way). Luggage fees add up — carry-on only when possible.
- **Southeast Asia:** AirAsia, Scoot, VietJet, Citilink for budget regional flights ($15–60 one-way).
- **Latin America:** LATAM, Avianca, Sky Airline, Gol for regional connections.
- **North America:** Southwest (no change fees, 2 free bags), Spirit/Frontier (ultra-budget, everything extra), JetBlue, WestJet (Canada).

## Rail Travel

### Europe's Rail Network
Europe has one of the world's best high-speed rail networks.
- **Eurail Pass:** Allows unlimited travel across 33 countries. Best value for travelers visiting 4+ countries. Book seat reservations separately (required on high-speed trains, €5–15 per reservation).
- **Point-to-point tickets:** Often cheaper than Eurail for 1–2 country trips. Book on national rail websites (SNCF for France, Trenitalia for Italy, Renfe for Spain, Deutsche Bahn for Germany).
- **High-speed times:** Paris–London (Eurostar, 2h20m), Paris–Amsterdam (Thalys/Eurostar, 2h20m), Paris–Barcelona (4h15m), London–Edinburgh (4h30m).

### Asia Rail
- **Japan:** JR Pass covers most Shinkansen lines. Purchase before arriving in Japan.
- **China:** Extensive high-speed rail network (350km/h). Book tickets on 12306.cn or via third-party (Trip.com). Required to present passport at the station.
- **India:** Indian Railways is one of the world's largest networks. Book 60–90 days in advance on IRCTC (hard to register for foreigners — use Cleartrip or a travel agent). Overnight sleeper trains are a classic India experience.

### Americas Rail
- **USA Amtrak:** Limited network but scenic routes. Northeast Corridor (Boston–NYC–Washington) is practical. The California Zephyr (Chicago–San Francisco, 2 days) and Empire Builder (Chicago–Seattle/Portland) are iconic.
- **Canada VIA Rail:** The Canadian (Toronto–Vancouver, 4 days) is a bucket-list journey.
- **South America:** Peru Rail/Inca Rail (Cusco–Machu Picchu) are the most important. Long-distance buses (Cruz del Sur, Oltursa in Peru; Turbus in Chile) are more practical than trains for most South American routes.

## Bus Travel

### When Buses Make Sense
- Short distances within countries (1–5 hours)
- Budget destinations where bus is significantly cheaper than flying
- Scenic routes where the journey itself is the experience

### Regional Bus Networks
- **Europe:** FlixBus ($5–40) connects 40+ countries. Slower than trains but great for budget travelers. Good for routes trains don't cover well.
- **Southeast Asia:** Night buses between major cities are economical ($5–15). Book through 12GoAsia or directly at bus stations.
- **Latin America:** First-class buses (Cruz del Sur, Tepsa, Oltursa) offer flat beds on overnight routes. Standard for backpacker travel.

## Local Urban Transport

### Metro/Subway Systems
Major city metro systems are almost always the cheapest and fastest way to navigate. Get rechargeable cards where available:
- **London Oyster Card / Contactless:** Cap on daily spending, much cheaper than single tickets.
- **Tokyo IC Cards (Suica/Pasmo):** Works on trains, subways, and buses. Buy at airport.
- **Paris Navigo:** Weekly or monthly pass covers all zones.
- **NYC MetroCard / OMNY Contactless:** 30-day unlimited pass for heavy use.
- **Bangkok BTS Rabbit Card / MRT:** Stored value for skytrain and metro.

### Ride-Hailing Apps by Region
- **Global:** Uber (operates in 70+ countries)
- **Southeast Asia:** Grab (Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, Vietnam, Philippines, Indonesia)
- **Latin America:** Uber, Cabify, InDriver, 99
- **China:** DiDi (Uber does not operate in China)
- **India:** Ola, Uber
- **Middle East:** Careem (Uber subsidiary)

### Car Rental Tips
- **When to rent:** Rural areas, national parks, road trips, destinations with no public transport.
- **Drive on the left:** UK, Ireland, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, South Africa, India, Thailand.
- **International Driving Permit (IDP):** Required in many countries outside your license's home country. Get from your national automobile association (AAA in USA, CAA in Canada).
- **Insurance:** Check if your credit card offers collision damage waiver (CDW). Decline the rental company's expensive CDW if so. Purchase third-party liability separately if needed.
- **Fuel:** "Petrol" (UK/Australia) = gasoline. Know whether your car is petrol, diesel, or electric before fueling.

## Ferry & Water Transport

### Key Ferry Routes
- **Greek Islands:** Piraeus (Athens) to Santorini, Mykonos, Crete, and Rhodes. Book via Ferryhopper or directly with Blue Star Ferries.
- **Croatia:** Split to Hvar, Korčula, Dubrovnik. Jadrolinija is the main operator.
- **Scandinavia:** Copenhagen to Oslo (Dfds), Stockholm to Helsinki (Viking Line/Tallink Silja) — overnight ferries with cabins.
- **Italy:** Civitavecchia (Rome) to Sardinia; Bari to Dubrovnik; Naples to Capri (hydrofoil, 45 min).
- **Southeast Asia:** Thai islands (various operators), Bali to Lombok/Gili Islands.
- **Alaska Marine Highway:** Ferry system connecting coastal Alaska communities — scenic and unique.

## Multi-Modal Journey Planning
For complex itineraries combining flights, trains, ferries, and local transport:
- **Rome2Rio:** Shows all route options between any two points worldwide with cost estimates.
- **Google Maps:** Best for local navigation in real time. Works offline (download maps in advance).
- **Omio:** Aggregates trains and buses across Europe for easy booking.
- **12GoAsia:** Southeast Asia transport booking (buses, trains, ferries, transfers).
