# Australia Travel Guide

## Overview
Australia is a continent-sized country of dramatic contrasts — the Great Barrier Reef, the red rock formations of the Outback, pristine white-sand beaches, lush rainforests, and cosmopolitan cities like Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane. Its sheer size means travelers must be selective, as distances between attractions are vast.

## Top Destinations

### Sydney
Australia's largest and most iconic city. The Sydney Opera House (tours available, or attend a performance) and Harbour Bridge (climb available, or walk the Cahill Expressway for free views) are world-famous landmarks. Bondi Beach is a 30-minute bus ride from the city; the Bondi to Coogee coastal walk is spectacular. The Rocks is Sydney's historic precinct. Taronga Zoo offers stunning harbor views alongside native wildlife. The Blue Mountains (1.5 hours west) with the Three Sisters rock formation are an excellent day trip.

### Melbourne
Australia's cultural capital, often ranked among the world's most livable cities. The CBD's laneways (Hosier Lane, Centre Place) are filled with street art, cafés, and boutiques. Melbourne's food scene is outstanding — Federation Square, South Yarra, Fitzroy, and Collingwood are dining hubs. The Great Ocean Road day trip (or multi-day drive) features the Twelve Apostles limestone stacks. Phillip Island's penguin parade is a popular evening excursion.

### The Great Barrier Reef
The world's largest coral reef system, off the coast of Queensland. Cairns and Port Douglas are the main gateways. Day snorkel/dive trips depart daily from Cairns marina. Liveaboard dive trips allow more remote reef access. The outer reef has the healthiest coral. The Whitsunday Islands (accessible from Airlie Beach) offer sailing and stunning Whitehaven Beach.

### Uluru (Ayers Rock)
Sacred to the Anangu Aboriginal people, the monolithic sandstone rock in the Northern Territory is one of Australia's most powerful landscapes. Climbing Uluru is now permanently prohibited out of respect for traditional owners. Sunrise and sunset viewing spots, the Uluru base walk (10.6km), and the Kata Tjuta (the Olgas) are the key experiences. Stays in Yulara (Ayers Rock Resort) are expensive — book well ahead.

### Tasmania
Australia's island state (1-hour flight from Melbourne) is 40% national park. The Tasmanian Wilderness World Heritage Area includes Cradle Mountain, Lake St Clair, and the Overland Track (6-day hike). Hobart's MONA (Museum of Old and New Art) is one of the world's most remarkable private museums. The Bay of Fires has some of Australia's most beautiful beaches.

## Transportation
- **Domestic flights:** Qantas, Virgin Australia, and Jetstar connect all major cities. Flights are the only practical option for long distances — Sydney to Perth by car is 4,000km.
- **Trains:** The Indian Pacific (Sydney–Perth, 4 days) and The Ghan (Adelaide–Darwin, 2 days) are iconic scenic rail journeys. Not practical for regular travel.
- **Car rental:** Essential for the Great Ocean Road, road trips along the coast, and rural exploration. Drive on the left.
- **Buses:** Greyhound Australia covers the East Coast. Popular with backpackers doing the Sydney–Cairns route.

## Budget Estimates (per day)
- Budget: $80–120 AUD (~$52–78 USD) — hostel dorms, supermarket meals, free beaches and parks
- Moderate: $180–280 AUD (~$117–182 USD) — mid-range hotels, café breakfasts, pub dinners, day tours
- Luxury: $400+ AUD (~$260+ USD) — boutique hotels, fine dining, helicopter tours, liveaboard reef trips

## Best Time to Visit
Australia is in the Southern Hemisphere, so seasons are reversed from North America and Europe.
- **Sydney/Melbourne (Southeast):** September–November (spring) and March–May (autumn) are ideal. December–February (summer) is beach season but can be very hot.
- **Great Barrier Reef/Cairns:** May–October (dry season, less humidity, clearest water).
- **Uluru/Outback:** April–September (cooler months). Summer (December–February) reaches 45°C+.
- **Tasmania:** December–March (summer) for hiking. Winter is cold and many trails are inaccessible.

## Visa & Entry
Most visitors need an Electronic Travel Authority (ETA, subclass 601) or an eVisitor (subclass 651). These are inexpensive ($20 AUD for ETA) or free (eVisitor for EU citizens) and applied for online before travel. Citizens of the USA, Canada, and most EU countries are eligible for one of these. New Zealand citizens can enter visa-free indefinitely.

## Cultural Tips
- Australia is a large, multicultural country — don't expect a uniform culture.
- The Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples have over 65,000 years of continuous culture — approach sacred sites respectfully.
- Sun protection is critical: Australia has the world's highest rates of skin cancer. Apply SPF 50+, wear a hat.
- Wildlife is often dangerous: jellyfish, crocodiles (Northern Australia), spiders, and snakes. Swim at patrolled beaches between the flags.
- Tipping is not expected but appreciated for exceptional service (10% is generous).
- Tap water is safe to drink everywhere.
