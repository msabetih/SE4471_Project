# Canada Travel Guide

## Overview
Canada is the world's second-largest country by area — a land of stunning natural diversity spanning Rocky Mountain peaks, Arctic tundra, Great Lakes, Atlantic fishing villages, and cosmopolitan cities. Its vast distances mean regional travel planning is essential, but each region rewards deeply.

## Top Destinations

### Toronto
Canada's largest city is one of the world's most multicultural. The CN Tower (observation deck and EdgeWalk) is the signature landmark. Kensington Market and Chinatown are vibrant multicultural neighborhoods. The Distillery District has Victorian industrial architecture converted to galleries and restaurants. Niagara Falls is 1.5 hours by car or bus — do both the Canadian side (much better views) and consider a Hornblower boat cruise. The Royal Ontario Museum is excellent.

### Vancouver
Set between mountains and the Pacific, Vancouver is consistently ranked among the world's most beautiful cities. Stanley Park (1,000-acre forest with seawall cycle/walk) is unmissable. Granville Island Public Market is the best food market in Canada. The Capilano Suspension Bridge and Grouse Mountain are popular. Whistler (2 hours north by Sea-to-Sky Highway) is one of North America's top ski resorts and excellent for mountain biking in summer.

### The Canadian Rockies (Banff & Jasper)
Among the most spectacular mountain landscapes on Earth. Banff National Park: Lake Louise (emerald glacial lake, château hotel, hiking), Moraine Lake (even more photogenic — get there before 6am in summer), the Icefields Parkway (230km scenic drive), and Banff townsite. Jasper National Park: Athabasca Glacier, Maligne Lake, and some of the best dark skies in North America. Calgary is the nearest major airport (90 minutes to Banff).

### Montreal
Canada's French-speaking heart. Old Montreal (Vieux-Montréal) has beautiful 17th–19th century architecture and the Notre-Dame Basilica (book tickets for the Aura light show). The Plateau-Mont-Royal neighborhood is the city's bohemian soul — Saint-Denis and Mont-Royal Avenue are excellent for cafés and restaurants. Montreal has a legendary food scene: bagels (St-Viateur vs. Fairmount is a local debate), smoked meat (Schwartz's), poutine, and high-end French cuisine. The Jazz Festival (late June/early July) and Just for Laughs comedy festival (July) are world-famous.

### Quebec City
The only walled city in North America outside of Mexico. Old Quebec (Vieux-Québec) is a UNESCO World Heritage site of cobblestone streets and fortified walls. The Château Frontenac is one of Canada's most photographed buildings. The Plains of Abraham (site of the 1759 battle that decided Canada's future) is now a large urban park. The Winter Carnival (February) is the world's largest winter outdoor festival.

## Transportation
- **Domestic flights:** Air Canada, WestJet, and Porter Airlines. Essential for coast-to-coast travel. Toronto to Vancouver is 5 hours (4,400km).
- **VIA Rail:** Train network connects major cities. The Canadian (Toronto–Vancouver, 4 days) is an iconic journey but impractical for itinerary travel.
- **Car rental:** Essential for the Canadian Rockies, Maritime provinces, and rural Canada. Drive on the right.
- **Public transit:** Toronto (TTC), Vancouver (TransLink/SkyTrain), and Montreal (STM Metro) have reliable urban transit. Other cities are very car-dependent.

## Budget Estimates (per day)
- Budget: $80–120 CAD (~$58–88 USD) — hostels, grocery store meals, free parks and trails
- Moderate: $180–280 CAD (~$130–205 USD) — mid-range hotels, sit-down restaurants, paid attractions
- Luxury: $400+ CAD (~$290+ USD) — mountain lodge hotels, fine dining, heli-skiing, guided wilderness tours

## Best Time to Visit
- **Canadian Rockies:** June–September for hiking (snow-free trails). Moraine Lake access: early June–mid-October. Ski season: November–April.
- **Vancouver/Victoria:** April–October (relatively dry). May–June: best balance of weather and crowds.
- **Toronto/Montreal:** May–October. Montreal winters are severe but festive.
- **Quebec City:** December–March for Winter Carnival and snow. Summer (June–August) for festivals.
- **Northern Canada/Aurora Borealis:** Yukon and Northwest Territories, September–March for Northern Lights viewing.

## Visa & Entry
Citizens of 53 countries (including the USA, EU, UK, Australia) can visit Canada visa-free. USA citizens only need a valid passport or NEXUS card. Citizens of other eligible countries need an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA, CAD $7) applied for online before flying. Some nationalities require a full visitor visa from a Canadian embassy.

## Cultural Tips
- Canada is bilingual (English and French) — in Quebec, French is the official language. Learning basic French phrases is appreciated in Quebec.
- Tipping: 15–20% at restaurants is standard. $1–2 CAD per drink at bars, 10–15% for taxis.
- Healthcare is publicly funded for Canadians — travelers must have travel health insurance.
- Wildlife safety: In the Rockies, carry bear spray, make noise on trails, and never feed or approach wildlife.
- Canada is very LGBTQ+-friendly, especially in major cities. Vancouver's West End and Toronto's Church-Wellesley Village are historic LGBTQ+ neighborhoods.
