# Spain Travel Guide

## Overview
Spain is a country of passionate culture, stunning architecture, world-class cuisine, and remarkable regional diversity. Each region — Catalonia, Andalusia, the Basque Country, Castile — feels almost like a separate country with its own language, food, and traditions. Spain is also one of Europe's most affordable major destinations.

## Top Destinations

### Barcelona
Catalonia's capital is defined by Antoni Gaudí's extraordinary architecture. La Sagrada Família is the most visited attraction in Spain — book timed-entry tickets months in advance. Park Güell, Casa Batlló, and Casa Milà (La Pedrera) are other Gaudí masterpieces. Las Ramblas is tourist-heavy but worth a stroll. El Born and Gràcia neighborhoods are better for authentic tapas bars. Barceloneta Beach is central and walkable.

### Madrid
Spain's capital is a city of great art museums and late-night energy. The "Golden Triangle of Art": the Prado (Velázquez, Goya, El Greco), the Reina Sofía (Picasso's Guernica), and the Thyssen-Bornemisza. El Retiro Park is beautiful for a morning walk. Madrid's nightlife starts late — dinner at 10pm, clubs from 2am are normal. The Mercado de San Miguel is a popular food market.

### Seville
The soul of Andalusia. The Real Alcázar (Moorish royal palace, still in use by the royal family) and the Gothic Cathedral (with Columbus's tomb) are unmissable. The Barrio Santa Cruz (old Jewish quarter) has labyrinthine streets and tapas bars. Flamenco is authentic here — attend a live show at a tablao. Seville is extremely hot in July–August (40°C+); spring and autumn are ideal.

### Granada
The Alhambra palace and Generalife gardens are among the most beautiful Moorish architectural achievements in the world — book tickets weeks or months ahead. The Albaicín (old Moorish quarter) has views of the Alhambra from its tea houses. Granada is one of the few cities where tapas are still free with drinks.

### San Sebastián (Donostia)
A small city in the Basque Country with arguably the best food concentration per capita in the world. The pintxos (Basque tapas) bars in the Parte Vieja (old town) are extraordinary — Gros and Centro neighborhoods have excellent options. La Concha beach is one of Europe's most beautiful city beaches. Easy day trip to Bilbao (Guggenheim Museum) by bus.

## Transportation
- **AVE (high-speed train):** Connects Madrid to Barcelona (2h40m), Seville (2h30m), Valencia, and Málaga. Book via Renfe (renfe.com) — advance booking saves significantly.
- **ALSA buses:** Extensive national coach network, cheaper than trains for some routes.
- **Metro:** Madrid and Barcelona have excellent metro systems. Barcelona's T-Casual card (10 journeys) is good value.
- **Car rental:** Useful for Andalusia countryside, Castile road trips, and rural areas.

## Budget Estimates (per day)
- Budget: €45–70 — hostels, menú del día (3-course lunch with wine for €10–15), free pintxos browsing in San Sebastián
- Moderate: €100–160 — mid-range hotels, restaurant dinners, paid museum entries
- Luxury: €250+ — boutique paradores (historic hotels), fine Basque cuisine, private flamenco shows

## Best Time to Visit
- **Spring (March–June):** Best overall. Semana Santa (Holy Week) in Seville is extraordinary but extremely crowded.
- **Autumn (September–October):** Excellent weather, grape harvest in Rioja and Penedès wine regions.
- **Summer (June–August):** Coastal areas are packed. Inland cities (Seville, Córdoba) are brutally hot. San Sebastián and the north are milder.
- **Winter (November–February):** Madrid and Barcelona are mild and far less crowded. Ski season in Sierra Nevada and Pyrenees.

## Visa & Entry
EU citizens travel freely. Non-EU visitors from the USA, Canada, UK, Australia, and most Western countries can visit Spain (Schengen Area) visa-free for up to 90 days within any 180-day period. ETIAS authorization will be required starting 2025.

## Cultural Tips
- Lunch (14:00–16:00) is the main meal; restaurants rarely open for dinner before 20:30–21:00.
- Siesta is still observed in smaller towns — shops may close 14:00–17:00.
- Tipping: not obligatory but rounding up or leaving €1–2 is appreciated.
- Spaniards are warm and social; don't be surprised by loud, animated conversation — it's enthusiasm, not arguing.
- Learn a few words of Spanish (and Catalan in Barcelona, Basque in San Sebastián) — locals appreciate the effort.
