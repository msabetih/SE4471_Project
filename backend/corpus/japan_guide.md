# Japan Travel Guide

## Overview
Japan is one of Asia's most captivating destinations, blending ancient tradition with cutting-edge modernity. The country spans four main islands — Honshu, Hokkaido, Kyushu, and Shikoku — and offers experiences ranging from serene temple gardens to bustling neon-lit cities.

## Top Destinations

### Tokyo
Japan's capital is a megacity of neighborhoods. Shibuya and Shinjuku offer world-famous nightlife and shopping. Asakusa preserves old Edo-era Tokyo with its Senso-ji Temple and traditional craft markets. Harajuku is the center of Japanese street fashion. The Tsukiji Outer Market and Toyosu Market are essential stops for sushi lovers.

### Kyoto
Former imperial capital and the heart of traditional Japan. Must-visit sites include Fushimi Inari Shrine (thousands of torii gates), Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion), and the Gion district for geisha culture. Kyoto has over 1,600 Buddhist temples and 400 Shinto shrines.

### Osaka
Japan's kitchen and comedy capital. Dotonbori canal area is famous for street food — takoyaki (octopus balls), okonomiyaki (savory pancakes), and kushikatsu (skewered fried food). Osaka Castle is a major landmark. Day trips to Nara (deer park and giant Buddha) are easy from Osaka.

### Hiroshima & Miyajima
The Peace Memorial Park and Museum offer a sobering and important historical experience. Miyajima Island's floating torii gate of Itsukushima Shrine is one of Japan's most iconic images.

## Transportation
- **Shinkansen (bullet train):** The fastest way to travel between cities. A 7-day Japan Rail Pass (~¥50,000) covers most routes including Tokyo–Kyoto–Osaka–Hiroshima.
- **IC Cards (Suica/Pasmo):** Rechargeable cards work on subways, local trains, and buses in most cities. Also accepted at convenience stores.
- **Subway:** Tokyo and Osaka have extensive, reliable metro networks. Google Maps works well for navigation.

## Budget Estimates (per day)
- Budget traveler: ¥5,000–8,000 (~$35–55 USD) — hostels, convenience store meals, free temples
- Moderate: ¥12,000–20,000 (~$80–135 USD) — mid-range hotels, sit-down restaurants, paid attractions
- Luxury: ¥40,000+ (~$270+ USD) — ryokan (traditional inn), kaiseki dining, private tours

## Best Time to Visit
- **Spring (March–May):** Cherry blossom season (sakura), most popular period — book months in advance.
- **Autumn (October–November):** Fall foliage (koyo), comfortable temperatures, fewer crowds than spring.
- **Summer (June–August):** Hot and humid, typhoon risk in August/September. Festivals (matsuri) are vibrant.
- **Winter (December–February):** Snow in Hokkaido and Japanese Alps; onsen (hot spring) season. Tokyo is mild.

## Visa & Entry
Citizens of over 60 countries (including USA, Canada, UK, EU, Australia) can enter Japan visa-free for up to 90 days for tourism. A valid passport and return ticket are required. No vaccination requirements as of 2024.

## Cultural Tips
- Remove shoes when entering homes, ryokan, and many traditional restaurants.
- Tipping is not practiced and can be considered rude.
- Carry cash — many smaller establishments and rural areas are cash-only.
- Onsen (hot spring baths) typically prohibit tattoos.
- Eat and drink while walking is frowned upon; find a spot to stop instead.
